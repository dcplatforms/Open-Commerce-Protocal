Open Commerce Initiative (OCI)

The Open Commerce Initiative (OCI), powered by the Open Commerce Protocol (OCP), is the standard for Agentic Commerce. It gives product, design, and engineering teams a single surface to build autonomous wallet systems—enabling A2A payments, UCP intents, and Web3 vaulting.

Agentic Commerce Catechism

Questions and answers on the new standard

Q1. What is Agentic Commerce?
Commerce where autonomous AI agents negotiate and settle transactions directly. OCP provides the standardized protocol (UCP) and secured identity layer for these agents to transact trustlessly.
Q2. Why is adoption accelerating?
Wallets captured 50% of global ecommerce in 2023 (FIS Global Payments Report) and are pacing toward 61% by 2027, pushed by mobility apps, super-app ecosystems, and cross-border loyalty.
Q3. How does Web3 fit in?
OCP includes a PCI-compliant vault for private keys. Agents can generate blockchain wallets and sign transactions securely inside the vault ("enclave signing"), enabling on-chain settlement without risk.
Q4. What trends inform the roadmap?
Auto top-up triggers, shared wallets between partners, and ledger-to-ledger settlement for subscription or mobility networks. Each line item shows up as an issue or example in the repo.

Strategic Overview

What the platform solves

OCP lets teams spin up dedicated wallets for AI agents, enforce spending policies, and execute standard commerce intents (UCP). The approach unifies traditional payments, agent-to-agent transfers, and blockchain settlement in one secure layer.

  • Wallet adoption: FIS reports digital wallets now represent half of global ecommerce and are tracking toward 61% by 2027, so internal ledgers are no longer optional.
  • Stored-value float: MarketWatch notes Starbucks holds over $1.8B in customer deposits, showing how closed-loop wallets finance working capital.
  • Compliance leverage: Basis Theory’s PCI scope study shows merchants can move up to 93% of PCI controls to a vault provider by never touching card data directly.
